During my time outside of the classroom, I’ve developed a passion for building cross-platform applications & games. My GitHub houses source code belonging to ten of these prototypes, two of which have been previously released onto the Google Play store under the brand name Lit Lab Productions.

In exchange for countless nights of curiosity, I've gained the irreplaceable quality of self-reliance. As the sole provider of all development in its entirety, I’ve never once found a problem that couldn't be solved. From design to polish, my true passion is scattered throughout the game development process.

Most recently, I’ve been investing a significant amount of time into polishing a couple projects. The first of which, Roost is a physics-based motocross sandbox meant to showcase my upcoming asset, Dave’s Precision Systems, prior to its release onto the Unity Asset Store.

D.P.S. provides balance, suspension, engine, transmission, camera and sound systems for two & four-wheel vehicles. The Roost vertical slice (proof of concept), which demonstrates the physics systems provided in this public asset is expected to launch by the end ofJuly. Roost also features a fully custom color system, allowing players to customize an eight-piece dirt bike with their choice of either pre-colored textures or by using the color-chooser tool to define a hex color.

Additionally, I’ve been developing Polygonic Crusade, a top-down, R.T.S. that allows for both single player and online multiplayer matches. Inspired by games like Age of Empires, my intention is combining original RTS gameplay with modern mobile controls found in applications like YouTube.
